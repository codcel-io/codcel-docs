## COSH Function

The `COSH` function in Excel returns the hyperbolic cosine of a number. Hyperbolic functions, like `COSH`, often appear in mathematical analyses of certain phenomena in the natural and engineering sciences.

### Syntax:

    COSH(number)

- **number**: The value for which you want the hyperbolic cosine.

### Examples:

1. `=COSH(0)` would return `1`, since the hyperbolic cosine of 0 is 1.
2. `=COSH(1)` returns a value approximately equal to `1.54308`.

> **Note**: Hyperbolic trigonometric functions like `COSH` are related to, but distinct from, their circular counterparts (like `COS`). They have various applications in mathematics, physics, and engineering, especially in contexts like the study of hyperbolic geometry or in representing certain types of waveforms.
